Why Sail to Isla Mujeres?


Imagine arriving for first time to a dreamy island, with white sandy beaches and stunning turquoise waters, only to find many woman-shaped sculptures and colorful offerings. That was what Spanish conquerors saw first and eventually led to the name of this gorgeous place, Isla Mujeres.


Isla Mujeres literally means Island of Women and its magical landscapes with picturesque cliffs kissed by a wild sea were once a place to worship Ixchel, the Mayan fertility and moon goddess. Mayan women visited Isla Mujeres as a rite of passage to signal their coming into womanhood, pray for fertility and ask favors of the moon. This island is made of ancient magic and color.
